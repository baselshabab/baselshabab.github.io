About me

Investigating materials that protect subsurface energy systems

I am a Ph.D. student in Mining & Minerals Engineering at Virginia Tech, specializing in wellbore integrity and subsurface materials performance for underground hydrogen storage and energy-transition applications.

My research examines the mechanical, microstructural, and chemical evolution of Class H wellbore cement under high-pressure hydrogen exposure using field-retrieved formation waters.

I integrate experimental characterization—including porosity–permeability measurements, tensile and compressive strength testing, and fracture mechanics—with advanced analytical techniques such as SEM-EDS, XRD, XRF, TGA, ICP-MS, and micro-CT.

My broader goal is to understand cement degradation, pore network evolution, and hydrogen transport pathways that may affect legacy wells in depleted gas reservoirs.

I enjoy working across mining, petroleum, geomechanics, and materials-science disciplines to address technically complex energy challenges with rigorous experimental evidence.

Research areas

Core research themes

My research connects material behavior at the microstructural scale with the long-term performance of wells and subsurface storage systems.

01

Underground hydrogen storage

Evaluating material compatibility and transport behavior under high-pressure hydrogen exposure in depleted reservoir environments.

02

Wellbore integrity

Understanding how legacy well materials respond to new pressure, chemical, and operational conditions associated with subsurface energy storage.

03

Cement degradation

Investigating the mechanical, microstructural, and chemical evolution of Class H cement after exposure to hydrogen and formation waters.

04

Pore network evolution

Connecting changes in pore geometry, connectivity, porosity, and permeability with potential hydrogen migration pathways.

05

Rock–cement interfaces

Examining material interactions and interface performance that influence sealing behavior and subsurface containment.

06

CCUS and energy transition

Applying subsurface engineering principles to carbon sequestration, hydrogen storage, and responsible repurposing of existing infrastructure.
